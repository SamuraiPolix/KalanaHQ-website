KEY PRACTICES

BRINGING IN AGILITY WITH MVP DEVELOPMENT SERVICES

MVP software development shouldn’t be an arduous process, in fact it should be a seamless process which provides the end-user real value and ultimately, an efficient MVP.

Through our experience and hard work, our process has been refined over the years. What we now have is an efficient, condensed version of the MVP delivery model:

1. DISCOVERY & PROPOSAL

The discovery workshop defines the user experience, interface, tech architecture and much more. Cutting short the usual 2 weeks’ time, this is an intense 1-day workshop providing the client value for money and a shorter route to idea realisation.

2. SALES

Once the idea is realised, we progress to the contract stage and agree on the scope and cost.

3. KICK-OFF/SPRINT 0

The kick-off stage will consist of 2 separate kick-offs; one with the client and one with our internal team. This will essentially set expectations for both parties and set the project in motion.

4. ITERATION

We will use Agile and Scrumban methodology to start the project design and development process and ultimately the delivery the final product to the client.

5. FINAL RELEASE & CLOSURE

This will be where we release the final production build, handover it to the client and close the project.

6. SUPPORT & MAINTENANCE

At this stage, we provide operational and maintenance support according to the existing/new contract.

At Kalana, creating an MVP is a 3 months process, consisting of 6 sprints in total.

This can vary from project to project as it all depends on the technicality of the build. Each Sprint is essentially 2 weeks long and our agile, scrum teams ensures that each stage of the process is efficient.
